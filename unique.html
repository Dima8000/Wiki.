Notable unusual and historic vehicles

This page highlights automobiles that stand out because of their design, function, or history — from bubble cars to amphibious vehicles.

Microcars & Bubble cars

Microcars were popular in the post-war era as inexpensive personal transport. They are small, light, and uniquely charming.

  • Peel P50 — the smallest production car ever made.
  • BMW Isetta — bubble-shaped microcar with a front-opening door.

Amphibious cars

Amphibious cars can operate on land and water. While niche, they showcase engineering creativity.

  • Amphicar Model 770 — a production amphibious car from the 1960s.
